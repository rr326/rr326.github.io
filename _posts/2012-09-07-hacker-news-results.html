My last post got posted to Hacker News yesterday and it spent the day on the home page. As an obscure, no-follower blogger, I'm not used to any real traction, so I found the results pretty interesting. Here they are.

Posting to Hacker News - I tried to post directly, but I've only read HN and never posted / upvoted, so apparently my Karma wasn't high enough. My post got rejected. A friend was kind enough to post for me. (Thanks Nathan.)

It was posted at 10:30 am. Unfortunately my Google Analytics code was broken for 4.5 hours (after a Wordpress upgrade). So my stats are partial.

[caption id="attachment_290" align="alignnone" width="645"] Google Analytics Results[/caption]

 

Making some simple guesses as to missing data, I'd say I got over 5,000 page views - on a blog whose previous max was under 20. Interestingly, people seemed to really read it, with average time on page for the post at 4:34.

Just as interesting to me is what did NOT happen - very few people clicked to anything else on my blog:

 

[caption id="attachment_299" align="alignnone" width="579"] Page Views by Page[/caption]

I guess this makes sense - my post was basically a resource of pointers to tools. You didn't need to really trust me to get value from it, so there wasn't a lot of need to click on my About page. Also, since it wasn't an opinion piece, people didn't seem compelled to see what else I've written.

I also got 55 retweets, seeded by two friends and two Hacker News tweets (and the Hacker News community).

So, in summary:

  • Hacker News main page all day (starting at 10:30 am PT)
  • 86 upvotes ; 34 comments
  • 5,000 page views; 4 min time per page
  • Very little spillover to the rest of my blog

 

I'd say the "success", for what it was, was because it was on target for the HN community; was a good, practical set of resources, on a topic of current interest; and was written well, simply, and non-obnoxiously.
